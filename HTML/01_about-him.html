Spiritual: Stefan is currently the Pastor of PHANET Tertiary Institutions in Ghana. He loves the Lord and the Lord’s sheep. He is very passionate about providing sound spiritual mentorship to the youth, and training them in faith and the Lord’s love. He is a deacon of the Church of Pentecost. He lives by deep convictions of God’s Word, and is known for his ministry of the Word, healing, miracles, signs and wonders.
Academic: He holds an MSc in International Development from the University of Portsmouth, UK, where he was a Commonwealth Scholar. He graduated as the top student of his Master’s class, and one of the student speakers of the graduating class of 2022. He also holds a bachelor’s in Development Planning from KNUST where he was a Mastercard Foundation Scholar.
Family: Stefan is the first of 4 incredible brothers. He lived most of his life in Obuasi and Kumasi– where his parents currently live. He is not married but engaged to Ms. Ruthie Armah, a chemical engineer and data scientist.
Professional: Stefan Danquah is currently the strategic planning, monitoring and evaluation officer at the West African Centre for Cell Biology Infectious Pathogens (WACCBIP) and the Crick African Network research consortium. He also works as an independent impact consultant, who has led the evaluation of medium to large-scale projects of corporations and organizations including the GSMA Foundation, MTN Ghana, Sattva Consulting, Swedfund International, and Robert Smith Law Group, amongst many others. His experience cuts across a breadth of fields including research and training, youth-centred interventions, and international trade. Although Stefan has only three years of professional experience, his deep commitment to intrapreneurship and transformative leadership has propelled an acceleration in his career path. In the next ten years, he aspires to be an international development professional at the forefront of gender and youth policy in Africa.
